Apple Funnel Cake Recipe

Description

Apple Funnel Cake brings a delicious twist to a fairground favorite with the cozy flavors of homemade apple dessert. The batter starts light and airy — a mix of flour, eggs, milk, and a touch of sugar — then folded together with finely diced apples and a dash of cinnamon for warmth. Once poured into hot oil in a swirling pattern, the funnel cake fries to a perfect golden brown, crisp on the edges and soft in the middle, with bits of tender apple in every bite.

After frying, the cake is dusted generously with powdered sugar and a sprinkle of extra cinnamon. For an extra treat, drizzle it with caramel sauce or serve it warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream. This Apple Funnel Cake captures the sweet, comforting taste of autumn — a perfect dessert for fall festivals, family gatherings, or anytime you want a cozy homemade indulgence.

Ingredients

Batter

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon apple pie spice
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 3/4 cups milk
  • 1 cup applesauce
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup white sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon almond extract

Apple Topper

  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 2 pounds apples, peeled and chopped (about 4 cups)
  • 1/3 cup packed brown sugar
  • 2 teaspoons apple pie spice

Fry and Serve

  • 2 cups vegetable oil, or as needed, for frying
  • caramel-flavor ice cream topping, and/or powdered sugar, for serving

Steps

  1. Stir together flour, baking powder, apple pie spice, and salt in a bowl for the batter.
  2. Whisk together milk, applesauce, eggs, white sugar, and almond extract in a large bowl until combined. Add flour mixture and whisk until smooth. Batter should have the consistency of a cake batter and be thin enough to pass through a funnel.
  3. For apple topper, melt butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Add apples, brown sugar, and apple pie spice; cook, stirring occasionally, until apples are tender, 15 to 20 minutes. Remove from heat.
  4. Meanwhile, pour oil to a depth 1 1/2 to 2 inches in a deep, heavy- bottomed 8- to 10-inch skillet or deep fryer; heat to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).
  5. Put your finger over bottom opening of a funnel; fill funnel with 1/2 cup batter. Holding funnel close to surface of oil, release batter into oil while making a circular motion. Fry until golden brown, 1 to 2 minutes. Use tongs and a wide spatula to carefully turn funnel cake. If you don’t have a funnel, you can use a squeeze bottle instead. Fry until golden, about 1 minute more. Drain on paper towels. Repeat with remaining batter. Keep finished funnel cakes warm in a 180 degrees F (82 degrees C) oven while preparing remaining funnel cakes.
  6. Top with apple topper. Serve with caramel topping and/or powdered sugar.
